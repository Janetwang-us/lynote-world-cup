Will Messi, Ronaldo & Neymar Play in the 2026 World Cup?

All three icons are confirmed — but injuries, age, and squad selection have complicated the picture. Here's the latest update on each player heading into the tournament.

Updated June 5, 2026 · 6 min read · By Lynote
Messi and Ronaldo pictured together at a 2011 Portugal vs Argentina friendly — both now set for their record sixth World Cup. Photo: Wikimedia Commons, CC BY 3.0

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is finally here — and for millions of fans, the biggest question isn't which team will win. It's whether the three most iconic players of their generation will take the field one last time.

Messi. Ronaldo. Neymar. All three have defined modern football. All three are at the twilight of their careers. Here's what we know as of June 2026.

🇦🇷

Lionel Messi

Argentina · Age 38 · 6th World Cup
IN SQUAD — FITNESS BEING MONITORED
Messi in action at the 2018 FIFA World Cup — now preparing for his record sixth tournament. Photo: Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 3.0

Lionel Messi will play at the 2026 World Cup — making him the first men's player in history to compete at six different World Cups. Argentina coach Lionel Scaloni confirmed Messi in his 26-man squad and named him captain of the defending world champions.

The catch: Messi is carrying a hamstring injury. In late May, the 38-year-old left Inter Miami's match against Philadelphia Union in the 73rd minute with muscle fatigue in his left hamstring. Inter Miami confirmed the issue but provided no timetable for his return.

"We would have liked him to arrive without any kind of problems, but that is not the case. Our goal is to try to recover them and have them arrive in the best possible condition."

— Lionel Scaloni, Argentina head coach

As of early June, Messi has joined Argentina's training camp in Kansas City and is on an individual recovery programme. Argentina are expected to continue monitoring his fitness ahead of their group-stage opener against Algeria on June 16.

  • First men's player ever to appear in six World Cups
  • Defending champion — Argentina chasing back-to-back titles for the first time since 1962
  • Widely expected to retire from international duty after this tournament
  • Playing on home turf — he lives in Miami; tournament spans USA, Canada & Mexico

Cristiano Ronaldo

Portugal · Age 41 · 6th World Cup
IN SQUAD — FULLY FIT & TRAINING

Cristiano Ronaldo, now 41 years old, is in Portugal's 26-man squad — his record-equalling sixth World Cup tournament. He captains the side and has been training with the full group since camp opened.

This is almost certainly Ronaldo's last World Cup. And for the first time, Portugal arrives as a genuinely complete team — reigning UEFA Nations League champions, with a squad stacked with PSG's Champions League winners: Vitinha, Nuno Mendes, João Neves, and Gonçalo Ramos.

  • 143 international goals — the most in men's football history
  • 226 caps — also a world record
  • 940+ career goals for club and country — chasing the 1,000-goal milestone
  • Portugal drawn in Group K with DR Congo, Uzbekistan, and Colombia
  • Has never scored in a World Cup knockout stage match

The one thing missing from Ronaldo's trophy cabinet? The World Cup. This is his last real chance.

🇧🇷

Neymar Jr.

Brazil · Age 34 · Not selected
NOT SELECTED FOR BRAZIL'S 2026 SQUAD
Neymar at the 2018 World Cup — Brazil's all-time top scorer will not feature in 2026. Photo: Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 2.0

Neymar's 2026 World Cup dream is over. The 34-year-old has been plagued by injuries since tearing his ACL in October 2023 while playing for Al Hilal. He has barely played competitive football since, and Brazil's coach Dorival Júnior did not select him for the squad.

Brazil will instead rely on a new generation — Vinicius Jr., Rodrygo, Endrick, and Raphinha — to carry the Seleção through the tournament.

  • Not selected for Brazil's 2026 World Cup squad
  • ACL surgery October 2023 — almost no competitive football since
  • Brazil's all-time top scorer with 79 international goals
  • International career may now be effectively over at 34

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Messi playing in the 2026 World Cup?
Yes. Messi is in Argentina's 26-man squad and named captain. He is managing a left hamstring issue and his availability for the opening match on June 16 remains uncertain. He is on an individual recovery programme in Kansas City.
Will Ronaldo play in the 2026 World Cup?
Yes. Ronaldo, 41, is in Portugal's squad, fully fit, and training with the group. This is his sixth — and almost certainly final — World Cup.
Is Neymar in the 2026 World Cup?
No. Neymar was not selected by Brazil coach Dorival Júnior after two years of injury struggles following an ACL tear in October 2023.
Could Messi and Ronaldo face each other at the 2026 World Cup?
Yes — in theory. Argentina and Portugal could meet in the knockout stages. Reports suggest a possible quarter-final matchup in Kansas City if both teams advance as group winners.
Is this Messi's last World Cup?
It is widely expected that Messi will retire from international duty after this tournament. He has not officially confirmed this, but all indications point to 2026 being his farewell on the international stage.
